Introduction

These days English is dominating the world and also generally the performance of students heavily rely on practicing questions. Students need a sufficient amount of exam papers to practice for their exams. So, the exam papers are a very important educational resource for students. But if you consider Tamil language, there is a shortage of educational resources and exercise questions. At the same time other languages (eg; -English) have sufficient documents and Questions. Through our system students can translate English Language Question papers to Tamil Language Question papers in an easy way. In our research, we are going to build a neural machine translation model to develop English to Tamil translation based on Sri Lankas’ local exam paper questions. This framework will be an end-to-end web-based document translation platform. By our system teachers and students can upload scanned images of the English language question paper through our web system and get translated Tamil language question papers. .

Research Scope

Increasing cross-regional communication and information exchange has caused high demand in language translation. Language translation is not limited for any specific area. Scientific and technical documentations, instruction manuals, legal documents, textbooks, publicity leaflets, and newspapers are major documents which require translation. Machine Translation (MT) is the process of converting a text from a language (source language) into another language (target language) using automated system. The topic Machine Translation is not a new term in to the field. Research on machine translation has been started from 1940[1]. Warren Weaver introduced the usage of computers in natural language translation by inventing the phrase ‘computer translation’ in 1949[1].Sri Lanka has three main languages Sinhala, Tamil and English and it has high demand in language translation either by human or computer. The main reason for this is that many people do not have good knowledge in other languages than their mother language.

In Sri Lanka, most of the educational documents such as text books and exam papers are initially generated in English language then it is translated in to Tamil language. So, having an effective bilingual machine translation system developed based on educational domain will be a useful resource for all type of people engaged in education field.Sri Lankan developers have implemented many bilingual machine translation systems and many researches are ongoing. Some of them are domain based. For example a system has been implemented for Sri Lankan government documents domain.

A system developed for a domain can’t be used for another domain; it will result low accurate translations. Those domains are mainly concern about word-to-word or phrase-to-phrase translations.There are no domains to translate English language question papers to Tamil language question papers.Even google translator also doesn't have this function.A typical translation and a translation for a question paper are vastly different. Normal translation is concerned with the translation of words, phrases, sentences, or paragraphs.However, when translating a question paper, we must consider standard translation aspects such as question patterns, answer patterns, tables, graphics, scientific terminology, mathematical notations, and units, among other things.We attempt to overcome those issues through our system.

English to Tamil language translation poses the challenge of morphological and structural divergence. For instance,(i) the number of parallel corpora and (ii) differences between language, mainly the morphological richness and variation in word order due to syntactical divergence.Tamil languages suffers from both of these problems, especially when they are being translated from English.Moreover,Tamil language differ not only in word order but are also more agglutinative as compared to English which is fusional. For instance, English has Subject-Verb-Object (SVO) whereas Tamil has Subject-Object-Verb (SOV).While syntactic differences contribute to difficulties of translation models, morphological differences contribute to data sparsity.

Both NMT and SMT basically replace words with numbers. And mathematical calculations are performed on those numbers and translations is executed. Here, NMT replaces similar words by nearby numbers where as SMT replaces each word with random numbers. So NMT is more accurate in finding similar word translations. And also SMT is limited to how many words its Ngram model dictates while NMT evaluates fluency for the entire sentence as NMT model uses recurrent neural networks. So when a NMT system is well trained, it becomes much more efficient and effective than SMT model.
A significant number of significant assets are available online in English, which is regularly translated into native languages to facilitate knowledge exchange among locals who are not fluent in English. Manual translation, on the other hand, is a time-consuming, expensive, and tiresome procedure.To this aim, machine translation is a useful tool for converting text into a new language without the need for human intervention. Among all known machine translation systems, neural machine translation (NMT) is one of the most efficient translation approaches.

By eSTEP, we have applied NMT on one of the most morphological rich Indian language, i.e. English-Tamil. We proposed a novel NMT model using Multihead selfattention along with pre-trained Byte-Pair-Encoded (BPE) and MultiBPE embeddings to develop an efficient translation system that overcomes the OOV (Out Of Vocabulary) problem for low resourced morphological rich Indian language which do not have much translation available online.
There is a requirement for an end to end document translation of exam question papers between English and Tamil. This problem includes four challenges such as Image optimization for OCR, handling the parts of the question (diagrams, equations and tables), text extraction from images, converting unstructured text into structured form (well organized parallel corpus), creating a natural machine translation model, generation of translated document and creating web based platform for admin (team members) and clients(students).
In detail the image optimization for best OCR performance will include the following challenges such as converting the images to the optimum resolution, optimizing the Image contrast and sharpness, correcting the image angle, skew Correction, resolving keystore effect, correction of 3D perspective distortions, straightening of the lines, cropping unwanted areas of the images ,border removal, color inversion, noise removal, fixing the blurring, resolving exam question images with multiple columns and image despeckling.
Handling the parts of the question such as images, diagrams, tables, equations, and text format of the questions will be our next challenge. The object character recognition will only be applicable to convert the images with text into a text. And this process never represents the part of the questions mentioned above. So, handling the part of the question mentioned above is not an easy task. Before the ocr we better recognize these parts and remove it from the images for better ocr results. At the same time, we must maintain/store the location/position of those parts e.g., in which position the diagram was located in the question. Later we better extract the text in these parts of the question e.g., image captions. The training dataset (parallel corpus) must include all these question features. When we generate the final translated document, these parts must be included in the output document in the correct way as it is located in the source document.
Text extraction of a huge number of images is not a straightforward process. There are several challenges that have to be resolved such as batch processing, loading the images into ocr engine from a remote location, latency issues, language detection, text area detection, object character recognition, encoding and decoding the images and handling images with text belonging to multiple languages.
Converting unstructured text into structured form will be the next challenge after the object character recognition. Exam question text differs from regular text in many ways such as containing scientific terms, equations, diagrams, and tables so, converting the exam questions from unstructured text into structured form is not straightforward. Also, raw text extracted from examination question papers will includes a huge number of unwanted data /noises such as the text other than the questions, unwanted symbols, misinterpreted text, text belonging to image captions, text belonging to tables and equations and there will be a little chance to get the raw text with few mistakes. At the same time the conversion process of unstructured text to structured text must have some ability to maintain the original question structure such as such text of the question, questions order, and numbering system, sentence’s structure, answer structure, equations, diagrams, tables, etc.
Creating a natural machine translation model will be the next challenge we have. These days there are a variety of techniques and tools/sets of algorithms available for neural machine translation. We must evaluate each benchmark technique with our training dataset then we have to select the best performing algorithms to implement our final neural machine translation model. Later there will be a set of parameters to be tested with different combinations of values to get an optimum value of the parameters which generate the best performing model (hyper parameter optimization).
In end to end document translation generating the translated document with correct format as the source document will be a big challenge. It includes maintaining the text format of the source document, adding the part of the questions mentioned above in the right positions and generating a human readable file format such as PDF.
Main Objective: To develop a framework to translate the question paper from English to Tamil.
Sub Objective 1: Image optimization for OCR.
Sub Objective 2: Handling the parts of the question (diagrams, equations, and tables) and text formats & Text extraction from images.
Sub Objective 3: Creating a natural machine translation model & Generation of translated documents.
Sub Objective 4: Converting unstructured text into structured.
